We use job postings microdata from Lightcast (formerly Burning Glass Technologies) to study the impact of California, Colorado, New York, and Washington state's pay transparency law, which requires firms to publicly disclose wages in all vacancy postings, on the labor market. We find that before these laws were passed, only 24.0\% of vacancy postings had any wage information, of which only 31.1\% were point-wage offers (7.5\% of all postings). We find that treated states' share of vacancy postings with any wage information increased to 76.1\% after pay transparency was enacted. However, we also find that only 22.8\% of these postings advertised point-wage offers, and the median real salary range in treated states also widened by 3.9 percentage points.
